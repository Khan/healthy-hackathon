Uncover the secrets of hack week

What is hack week?

A whole week to build anything you want w/ friends.

Doesn't matter how wild or crazy your idea is. Team up, let loose, build absolutely anything you want that's related to KA. You have a whole week, dream big.

A chance to make, not just write code.

You don't have to be a coder! Hacking is making anything, not just coding. Quite frankly our past hackathons wouldn't have been half as fun if all corners of the company hadn't been such a big part.

Time to work both hard and healthy.

Like our hackathon, we'll work hard (office stays open late) while getting sleep and eating well. You're not gonna walk away lacking sleep and full of 5 hour energy shots.

Rules

  1. You have to create something connected to Khan Academy.
  2. You have to demo or show off something you've created on Hackaween.
  3. You have to act like a healthy hacker (sleep well, eat good food).

FAQ

Uh, I have critical work to do that week. And meetings with external partners. And a big deadline. And what if the site goes down?
We're going to push to keep calendars clear during hack week. But that doesn't mean these responsibilities aren't real. If you have external meetings, so be it. If the site goes down, we're gonna rush to fix it. If you have a critical deadline and you personally judge that hack week causes immense troubles, we trust your judgment.

But we strongly, strongly encourage folks to take advantage. Plan ahead. Clear calendars. Use hack week. It's once a year.

What are we hacking on?
Anything related to KA. Below is a grabbag of examples from previous hackathons, but now we have a whole week! Think big. The best ideas will come from you. Add yours here.

  • Ship the first multiplayer math game using KA's API.*
  • Dress up like Abe Lincoln and record growth mindset videos that get inserted into our tutorials.*
  • Master BigQuery enough to figure out if users from a specific non-US country have unique usage patterns, then blog about it.
  • Draw a mural that'll one day live on the walls of our new office.
  • Build a brand new exercise widget that teaches students how to read music, then make some content for it.*
  • Create your own tutorial full of videos and interactive content for learning sign language.
  • Write a blog post about what you do at KA.
  • Hack the next level of Tic-tac-toe-ception. “We need to go deeper.”*
  • Make your first perseus exercise.
  • Release a widget that lets anyone add Khan Academy profile flair to their sites/blogs. Then blog about it.
  • Design a sweet team t-shirt and get it printed for everyone before the first night ends.*
  • Learn any programming language you want by creating a wrapper for our API in said language. Chicken chicken chicken counts.
  • Add an API to our CS platform that lets users play sounds.
  • Hook up a robot that walks around the office and kicks people in the shins every time we cross another 1,000,000,000 problems done.
  • Add a donation banner to the top of every KA page that reads, “A personal appeal from Salman Khan.”*.
  • Ship a new careers page to the live site and a new build script that we’ve always wanted and a new embedded calculator for exercise users.*
  • Run your first A/B test. On what? You choose!

* Created during 2012 or 2013 hackathon

What's so different about our hack[athon| week]s?
This isn't just for 'coders'. Record videos. Draw a mural on our walls. Make a spreadsheet. Moderate our discussion boards. Write a KA song. As long as you’re creating something that you can show off to the rest of the group, you’re hacking and you’re in.

It’s healthy. We reject the whole red bull, 3am, alcohol-powered images of hackathons. We invest in employees for the long-term. We’ll eat well and get sleep.

K. What am I supposed to do before it starts?

  • Add your ideas to the Trello board
  • Commit to an idea if you’re sold.
  • Start lobbying others to join your team. We humbly suggest using this as a chance to work with people you don’t work with every day.
  • Do not start hacking yet. Cheater.

Who's invited?
KA employees, contractors, and close friends. Past KA interns. The whole KA Lite team. If you have a close friend who'd contribute and enjoy, feel free to invite 'em.

Who do I work with?
We highly encourage hackers to work in groups, but try to keep team size limited to 3 or 4 or maybe 5 or OK FINE 6 IS OK. Or go lone wolf.

Do I have to do this? Is the weekend required? FOR THE LOVE OF THE GODS I HAVE REAL WORK TO DO THIS ISN'T SUMMER CAMP BEN.
Much the same way nobody's required to win an olympic gold medal, nobody's required to participate in hack week. It just happens to be something that really impressive people try really hard to do.

Ok but seriously, no problem. First, the weekend is your time, you should use it without hesitation. Second, this is a completely optional hack week — if you have real external obligations or work milestones, so be it.
